Spring Simulation

Springs are a classic physics simulation used in many game systems. They are useful for modeling elastic movement, soft constraints, cloth simulation, and procedural animation.

The behavior of a spring is described by Hooke's Law. 

F = -k(x - L)

Where:

  • F is the force applied
  • k is the spring stiffness
  • x is the current length
  • L is the rest length

Interactive Simulation

Drag the mass to stretch the spring and watch it oscillate.

How the Simulation Works

The spring pulls the mass back toward its rest length. The further the mass moves from this rest position, the stronger the force becomes.

In simulations we also add damping to slowly reduce the energy of the system and prevent infinite oscillation. 

velocity += force
position += velocity
velocity *= damping

Where Springs Are Used in Games

  • Camera smoothing
  • Character animation
  • Cloth simulation
  • Soft body physics
  • Procedural movement
