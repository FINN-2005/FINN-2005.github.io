Vector Math and the Dot Product

Vectors are one of the most fundamental tools in game development. They are used everywhere: movement, rendering, lighting, physics, and collision detection.

A vector represents both direction and magnitude. In 2D space it can be written as: 

v = (x, y)

Interactive Simulation

Drag the endpoints of the vectors to explore how their relationship affects the dot product.

The Dot Product

The dot product measures how aligned two vectors are. 

dot = ax * bx + ay * by

If the dot product is positive, the vectors point in roughly the same direction.

If it is zero, the vectors are perpendicular.

If it is negative, the vectors point in opposite directions.

Why This Matters in Game Development

  • Lighting calculations
  • Checking if an enemy is in front of a player
  • Projection and reflection
  • Physics and collision detection
